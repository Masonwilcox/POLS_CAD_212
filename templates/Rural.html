Rural Perspectives in Canada: Navigating the Urban-Rural Divide

Canada is predominantly an urban nation, with 81% of its population living in urban areas as of 2011. This significant urban majority has profound implications for the country's rural regions, which often feel isolated from and exploited by their economic cores, such as Vancouver in British Columbia and Toronto in Ontario. As the rural population dwindles and the rural workforce shrinks, Canada has been compelled to look abroad for the resources once produced domestically. This shift has heightened concerns about external influences on Canadian politics and economy, underscoring the need for policies that bridge the urban-rural divide and strengthen national sovereignty.
